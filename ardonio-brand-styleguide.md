## Voice & Tone

The brand voice is that of a seasoned operator who has been in the room when hard decisions were made: direct, experienced, and without pretense.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Plain and direct.** No jargon. No corporate filler. Say the thing.
- **Earned confidence.** Claims are backed by real outcomes (revenue figures, exit multiples, deal names). Never vague.
- **Dry wit.** Occasional wry edge, but never at the expense of clarity.
- **Practitioner, not pundit.** Opinions come from doing, not observing.

**Headline style — lead with the hook:**
> *"Working with me is cheaper than the mistakes I'll help you avoid."*  
> *"Built, scaled, acquired, divested, exited."*  
> *"Paid the price, learned the lessons."*

**Avoid:**
- Superlatives without evidence ("world-class", "leading", "best")
- Passive voice
- Long preambles before the point
- Buzzword stacking

## Typography

### Typefaces

| Role | Font | Fallback |
|------|------|----------|
| **Primary (headings & body)** | Inter | -apple-system, Segoe UI, Roboto, Helvetica Neue, sans-serif |
| **Monospace / code** | JetBrains Mono | SFMono-Regular, Consolas, Menlo, monospace |

Inter is a free Google Font. Download at fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter.

### Type scale (web reference — scale proportionally for print/slides)

| Level | Size | Weight | Notes |
|-------|------|--------|-------|
| H1 / Slide title | 32px | 500 | Tight tracking (−0.03em) |
| H2 / Section heading | 22px | 500 | Tracking −0.02em |
| H3 / Subheading | 24px | 500 | |
| Body | ~16–17px | 400 | Line height 1.6 |
| Small / meta | 14px | 400 | Use Gray `#8A8D93` |
| Strong / bold | any | **700** | Use sparingly for emphasis |

**Letter spacing:** headings use slightly tighter tracking than default. Body uses −0.01em. Gives the type a clean, editorial feel.

## Layout Principles

- **White space is a feature.** Wide margins, generous paragraph spacing. Never crowd content.
- **Max content width: ~1040px equivalent.** In documents, keep text columns to roughly 65–75 characters wide.
- **Single column preferred** for long-form content. Two columns acceptable for service listings or side-by-side comparisons.
- **Left-aligned text** — no centring of body copy. Centring is for hero taglines only.

## Slide / Document Structure

### Cover / Title slide
- Company name: **Ardonio Ltd.**
- Subject line in large Inter 500
- Date or context line in Gray small text
- Optional: thin Blue (`#1E5BFF`) rule under the title

### Section divider
- Bold heading in Ink `#111214`
- Thin Blue left-border accent (like a blockquote)
- Minimal decoration

### Content slides / pages
- **One idea per slide.** If you have two ideas, make two slides.
- Lead with the *so what* — put the conclusion at the top, evidence below.
- Use bullet lists sparingly. Short sentences beat nested bullets.
- Prefer a strong single quote or stat over three mediocre bullets.

### Pull quotes / callouts
- Indent with a thin Blue `#1E5BFF` left border
- Slightly larger text (1.1–1.2× body size), weight 500
- Use for key insights or memorable phrases

## Iconography

The site uses **Font Awesome** solid and regular icon sets. For documents and presentations, keep icons simple and functional:

- Line icons preferred over filled where available
- Match icon colour to text or use Blue accent
- Keep icon size proportional to surrounding text (1×–1.5× cap height)

Common icons used:
- Envelope (mail / contact)
- Calendar (booking)
- Briefcase (services)
- Info circle (about)

## What to avoid

- Dark backgrounds (the brand is light/paper — use dark only for contrast moments)
- Multiple accent colours — Blue is the only accent
- Decorative fonts or script typefaces
- Dense slides with small text
- Jargon-heavy copy that obscures the outcome
